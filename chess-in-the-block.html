A book about people who learned strategy before peace.

This is not a chess manual. It is a human board: the pawn, the knight, the bishop, the rook, the queen, the king, and the cost of every move.

Inside the book

The pieces become people.

  • The Pawn: one step at a time.
  • The Knight: moving differently when no straight road exists.
  • The Bishop: faith, silence, and judgement.
  • The Rook: the ones who hold the line.
  • The Queen: invisible power.
  • The King: the weight of command.

Tone

Urban, reflective, direct.

  • Short sections with a spoken, intimate rhythm.
  • Family, class, pride, silence, grief, and emotional survival.
  • No cheap motivation. No romanticising pain.
  • Just the board seen clearly.
