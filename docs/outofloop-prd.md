# OutOfLoop PRD

## 1. Product Vision

OutOfLoop is a mobile app that helps people leave autopilot through real-world missions, safe circles, and human hosts who make sure nobody is left out.

The product is not a fitness app, productivity app, dating app, or traditional social network. It is a community engine: a way to create simple reasons for people, families, and groups to meet, reconnect, and live more stories together.

Core promise:

> Sai do automatico. Vive uma historia hoje.

Community promise:

> Ninguem fica de fora.

Human operating principle:

> Pessoas reais a tomar conta de pessoas reais.

## 2. Problem

Many people want more connection, novelty, courage, family time, and community, but their lives default to routine, screens, postponed plans, and vague intentions.

Existing products tend to over-index on:

- performance and streaks
- dating dynamics
- public feeds and attention
- expensive experiences
- passive event discovery
- broad social networks without real commitment

OutOfLoop solves a different problem: turning small intentions into safe, accessible, real-world action.

## 3. Target Users

Primary users:

- people who feel stuck in routine
- people who want to meet others without dating-app pressure
- families wanting better time together
- people who are alone or new in a city
- friends who want to do more than say "we should meet"
- parents looking for safe activities with children
- introverts who want gradual social confidence
- disabled people who want accessible, safe, realistic ways to join community life
- carers or family members who help someone participate in social activities

Community users:

- gyms
- football and sports groups
- schools and parent groups
- churches and faith communities
- disability associations and inclusive community groups
- local associations
- volunteering groups
- travel and day-trip groups
- small workplaces or clubs

## 4. Positioning

OutOfLoop is for people who want to live more stories.

It creates missions that fit the user's rhythm, energy, personality, availability, budget, and social comfort. It then helps those missions become plans with people.

Key differentiation:

- missions first, feed second
- private circles before public discovery
- low-cost by default
- safety and anti-bullying built in
- hosts, not influencers
- real-world action over online attention
- personalization by rhythm, feedback, and context

## 5. Core Product Loop

1. User opens the app.
2. User receives a personalized daily mission.
3. User can accept, swap, save, invite, or tap "Hoje nao da".
4. Mission can be done solo, with a pair, with family, with a circle, or as a group event.
5. If it becomes an event, people use "Quem alinha?" and accept before a timer expires.
6. The mission/event happens in the real world.
7. User checks out after completion.
8. User gives feedback.
9. User can share a memory with event participants.
10. App adjusts future missions and suggests the next connection.

## 6. Product Principles

- Nobody is shamed for not participating.
- Ignoring a mission is a signal, not a failure.
- Safety features are never premium-only.
- Low-cost and free options should be available whenever possible.
- Hosts exist to include, not to become influencers.
- Public discovery should grow only after private circles work.
- Children and family contexts require stricter privacy and moderation.
- Accessibility is a core inclusion requirement, not a future nice-to-have.
- The app should help people meet outside the screen, not keep them scrolling.

## 10. Mission Intensity

Every mission has one of three levels:

- Leve: 5-15 minutes, low effort
- Real: requires action but is accessible
- Coragem: creates a healthy "friozinho na barriga"

Examples:

- Leve: send a short message to someone you miss
- Real: invite someone for coffee this week
- Coragem: go to a public event and speak to one new person

## 12. "Hoje Nao Da"

This is a core feature.

When the user taps "Hoje nao da", the app asks:

> Sem stress. O que falhou hoje?

Options:

- sem tempo
- sem energia
- missao dificil demais
- nao era o tipo certo
- mau dia
- quero guardar para depois
- da-me uma mais leve

System response:

- sem tempo: suggest a 5-minute mission
- sem energia: suggest recomeço mission
- difficult: lower intensity
- wrong type: change category
- save: move to mission pool
- lighter: generate lighter alternative

Principle:

> The app pulls people out of autopilot, but never pushes them into shame.

## 13. Mission Pool

Users should have a personal mission pool for saved or dormant missions.

Mission states:

- suggested
- accepted
- saved
- arranging
- expired
- done
- paused

User actions:

- accept now
- save for later
- save for weekend
- do with someone
- turn into group mission
- reject with reason
- request easier version

Avoid language like "failed". Use expired, paused, or saved.

## 15. Events And "Quem Alinha?"

A mission can become an event.

Main button:

> Quem alinha?

Event fields:

- title
- description
- date/time
- public meeting location
- cost tag: free, low cost, medium, special
- minimum participants
- maximum participants
- acceptance deadline
- circle or community
- host
- helper, optional
- safety rules
- album/memories enabled

Event states:

- proposed
- open
- confirmed
- cancelled
- completed
- archived

Timer behavior:

- individual missions: short emotional commitment timer
- group/event missions: organizational acceptance deadline

Example:

> Caminhada sabado as 10h. Precisa de 3 pessoas ate sexta as 18h para confirmar.

If minimum is reached:

- event is confirmed
- participants receive details
- temporary event chat or updates area opens

If not reached:

- app suggests rescheduling
- app suggests converting to pair/solo mission
- app suggests a smaller low-cost alternative

## 17. Hosts

Hosts are not influencers. They are people who take care of other real people.

Host invitation:

> Tens ajudado outras pessoas a sair do loop. Queres tornar-te Anfitriao?

Trial invitation:

> Queres experimentar organizar uma missao de grupo esta semana?

Selection criteria:

- consistent participation
- good feedback from others
- few or zero reports
- inclusive behavior
- presence at events
- ability to organize
- respectful responses

Host levels:

- Ajudante: helps events and welcomes new members
- Anfitriao de Circulo: organizes inside a private group
- Anfitriao Local: organizes local open activities
- Anfitriao Verificado: passed verification, code of conduct, and positive history

Host protections and limits:

- hosts can be reported
- important decisions can be reviewed
- expulsions require reasons
- moderation actions are logged
- public events follow safety rules
- children/family groups have special rules
- hosts cannot access unnecessary private data

Host incentives:

- host badge
- organization tools
- beta feature access
- partner discounts
- free premium
- commission for approved paid events later
- local visibility

Avoid:

- follower counts
- public host rankings
- creator culture
- popularity competitions

## 18. Nobody Left Out

This is a design rule.

Product mechanics:

- events can reserve spaces for new members
- "vou sozinho/a" option to be welcomed by a host
- low-cost activities by default
- small groups for first encounters
- careful introductions for new people
- missions that encourage including someone
- host alert: "ha alguem novo neste evento"
- host alert: "3 pessoas nao participam ha algum tempo"

Host outreach templates:

- "Vamos fazer uma caminhada leve sabado. Gostavamos que viesses, sem pressao."
- "Ha um jantar low cost esta sexta. Queres alinhar?"
- "Lembramo-nos de ti para esta missao. Se fizer sentido, vem connosco."

Limits:

- no public exposure
- no repeated pressure
- user can opt out of this type of invitation
- maximum outreach frequency

## 19. Safety And Anti-Bullying

Safety is part of the core product.

Anti-bullying measures:

- code of conduct in every circle
- no public dislikes
- positive reactions only
- suspected aggressive comments trigger a warning before posting
- suspicious comments can be hidden pending review
- reporting on comments, events, photos, and users
- block and mute
- leave circle quietly
- hide personal stories
- private check-ins
- no "last place" or public failure rankings

Pre-post warning:

> Isto pode soar agressivo. Queres reformular?

Moderation:

- each circle has host/moderator
- larger groups can require approval to join
- code of conduct required
- report history
- temporary or permanent removal
- moderation action logs

Meeting safety:

- early meetings in public places
- group-first encounters for people meeting strangers
- verified host visible in event
- check-in "cheguei"
- check-out "ja sai"
- option to share plan with trusted contact later
- report/help button
- approximate location, not exact private location

Family/children safety:

- no direct child accounts in MVP
- family profiles controlled by adults
- stricter photo approval
- host approval for family events
- no 1:1 adult-child interactions

## 20. Accessibility And Disability Inclusion

"Nobody left behind" must include disabled people from the start.

The product should support people with:

- mobility disabilities
- visual impairments
- hearing impairments
- neurodivergence
- chronic illness
- anxiety or sensory sensitivity
- temporary injury
- carers or support companions

Accessibility principles:

- disabled users should not have to disclose details publicly to participate
- accessibility preferences are private by default
- event accessibility information should be visible before the user accepts
- hosts should be prompted to choose accessible locations when possible
- missions should have accessible alternatives
- low-energy and low-sensory missions should be available
- users should be able to bring a companion/carer when appropriate
- nobody should be pressured to explain why a mission is not possible

Onboarding accessibility preferences:

- step-free access needed
- seated activity preferred
- low-noise places preferred
- visual instructions helpful
- text-based communication preferred
- audio-based communication preferred
- easy transport/parking needed
- can walk short distances only
- needs companion/carer option
- prefers small groups
- prefers predictable plan and clear schedule

Event accessibility fields:

- step-free access
- accessible bathroom known/unknown
- seating available
- public transport nearby
- parking nearby
- noise level: quiet, moderate, loud
- walking distance: low, medium, high
- standing required: yes/no
- family-friendly
- support companion allowed
- accessibility notes

Mission adaptation examples:

- Standard: "Go for a 30-minute walk with someone."
- Accessible alternative: "Meet someone for a seated coffee or a short roll/walk in an accessible place."
- Standard: "Try a new gym class."
- Accessible alternative: "Do a movement session adapted to your body, alone or with someone."
- Standard: "Attend a busy local event."
- Accessible alternative: "Choose a smaller, quieter event or invite one person to a calm public place."

Host responsibilities:

- check accessibility basics before publishing an event
- clearly state what is known and unknown
- avoid promising accessibility that has not been verified
- welcome companions/carers without making it awkward
- avoid public attention around someone's disability
- offer an accessible meeting point
- ask privately if a participant requests help

Beta inclusion requirement:

- include at least 2-5 people with accessibility needs in the closed beta if possible
- include one disability-aware host/helper if possible
- test at least one accessible low-cost event
- ask specifically whether the app made participation easier or harder

## 21. Badges And Physical Identity

Physical badges should be for hosts/helpers only at first.

Purpose:

- make the official helper easy to identify
- increase trust at events
- support real-world safety

Possible text:

> Hoje sai do loop

Role line:

> Anfitriao

Future enhancement:

- QR code to verify that the host belongs to the event
- host kit for verified hosts

Avoid participant badges in MVP to reduce pressure and exposure.

## 22. Event Memories

Each event can have a private space for participants.

Possible names:

- Memorias
- Album
- Historias do Evento
- Momentos
- Depois da Missao

Recommended MVP name:

> Memorias

Access:

- only confirmed participants
- host and helpers
- admins if required for moderation

Memory types:

- photo
- short text
- later: audio

Post-event flow:

1. Check-out: "Como correu?"
2. Feedback: gostei / nao gostei / senti-me incluido/a / faria outra vez
3. Share memory: photo/text
4. Access event album
5. Suggestion: "Queres repetir com este grupo?"

Privacy:

- private by default
- no automatic public feed
- report/remove on each photo
- request removal if user appears in photo
- stricter review for children/family events
- option: "prefiro nao aparecer em fotos"

## 23. Feedback And Personalization

After a mission/event, ask minimal feedback:

- Gostaste?
- Foi facil, no ponto, ou demais?
- Farias outra parecida?
- Fizeste sozinho/a ou com alguem?
- Sentiste mais energia, conexao, coragem, calma?
- Queres mais missoes deste tipo?
- Sentiste-te incluido/a? for events

Signals used:

- explicit feedback
- accepted quickly
- saved
- ignored
- swapped
- completed
- tapped "Hoje nao da"
- event attendance
- check-out result
- preferred day/time
- mode
- budget
- family status
- group activity

Adaptive behavior:

- reduce intensity if missions are ignored or marked difficult
- suggest weekends if weekday missions are ignored
- suggest lighter missions during work-heavy days
- suggest group plans if user saves many social missions
- suggest host outreach if user wants company but does not initiate

## 24. Closed Beta

Closed beta is a critical phase, not an afterthought.

Goal:

Test whether OutOfLoop creates real participation, safety, and belonging with 20-50 people.

Target beta composition:

- 5-10 family/friend users
- 5-10 people who want to meet new people
- 2-5 people with accessibility needs, if possible
- 1 gym/sport group
- 1 church/community group
- 1 parent/family group
- 1 inclusive/disability-aware community contact, if possible
- 1 friends group
- 2-4 potential hosts

Beta duration:

- 4 weeks minimum
- 6 weeks preferred

Beta constraints:

- invite-only
- local geography where possible
- small groups
- host-led events
- low-cost/free events
- at least one accessibility-aware event
- manual support by founder
- no public discovery at first

What to test:

- onboarding completion
- mission acceptance
- "Hoje nao da" usage
- mission-to-event conversion
- event attendance
- host behavior
- safety and moderation
- whether people feel included
- whether low-cost matters
- whether accessibility information helps people decide safely
- whether people return after events
- whether people share memories

Key beta metrics:

- weekly active users
- mission acceptance rate
- mission completion rate
- saved mission rate
- "Hoje nao da" reasons
- event proposals created
- event confirmation rate
- event attendance rate
- repeat participation
- memories shared per event
- feedback: felt included
- accessibility friction reports
- reports/issues
- host satisfaction

Qualitative questions:

- Did this make you do something you would not have done?
- Did you feel safe?
- Did you feel included?
- Did the event feel accessible enough for you or someone you came with?
- Was the mission realistic for your day?
- Did the app create a real reason to meet?
- Would you invite someone else?
- What felt awkward?
- What should never happen here?

Beta success criteria:

- at least 60% of beta users accept one mission per week
- at least 30% attend or create one group activity during beta
- at least 50% of event participants submit feedback
- at least 70% of event participants say they felt included
- no unresolved serious safety issues
- at least 3 users naturally show host potential

## 28. Monetization Direction

Do not monetize safety, belonging, or basic inclusion.

Potential models:

- freemium users
- premium mission packs
- group pro plans
- gym/school/church/community plans
- local event partners
- low-cost experience marketplace later
- host kits and official badges
- municipal/community licenses

Best early bet:

> Communities that already exist but need activation: gyms, schools, churches, clubs, associations, and local groups.
