# CHAPTER 9

Celeste opened the blue envelope again.

The protection ended without a sound.

No light went out. No mechanism engaged. The house gave no sign that anything had changed.

It was only a paper flap lifted by one hand.

Duarte watched her.

“Do you confirm?”

Celeste removed the documents.

“I already opened it.”

“That wasn't what I asked.”

She raised her eyes.

“Yes.”

Duarte inclined his head.

“The protection has been revoked.”

Marcel gave a quiet laugh.

“It lasted less than five minutes.”

Celeste ignored him.

Adrian remained standing between his mother's chair and his father's place. Samuel stood by the sideboard, his daughter's photograph in one hand and Artur's box in the other. Tomás watched the blue envelope as though he might recognize the future of his own inside it.

Nico's chair was empty.

His glass remained beside the plate. A damp ring marked the tablecloth.

Celeste removed the photograph from the stage. Then the clipping. Then the transfers.

She knew those documents. Not all of them. Not in that form.

She turned the final page.

More sheets had been folded underneath.

Nico had not seen them.

Celeste looked at Duarte.

“He didn't reach the end.”

“No.”

“Why?”

“It wasn't necessary for the first choice.”

“Necessary for whom?”

“For the table.”

Celeste held up the pages.

“Not for him.”

Duarte did not answer.

Adrian moved closer.

“What's there?”

Celeste unfolded the first sheet.

A call log. Dates. Times. Unidentified numbers. Beside some of them were brief notes.

**INTERCEPTED**

**NOT DELIVERED**

**ANSWERED**

Celeste read the first page in silence.

On the second, a name appeared.

**RUI MENDES**

Adrian frowned.

“Who is he?”

Tomás answered.

“Nico's agent.”

Celeste turned the page. A payment table filled the top half. Rui Mendes appeared as an indirect beneficiary through a consulting firm. The source of the funds vanished behind two corporations and an investment fund.

But Celeste recognized one of them.

So did Marcel.

She looked at him.

“Larkstone.”

Marcel kept his expression still.

“I don't know it.”

“You do.”

“No.”

Celeste placed the page in front of him.

“Larkstone was used for external payments for nine years.”

“That doesn't mean I knew every beneficiary.”

“You signed the original authorization.”

“For communications services.”

“Communications.”

Marcel looked down at the documents.

“That's what it said.”

Duarte spoke.

“Rui Mendes was paid through companies connected to the Voss Group.”

Adrian turned to his father.

“To do what?”

Marcel answered, “I don't know.”

Duarte did not correct him.

Celeste noticed.

“You're not saying he's lying.”

“No.”

Marcel looked at him. The absence of accusation gave him confidence.

“You heard him.”

Duarte continued.

“Marcel authorized the operation. He did not manage the messages.”

The confidence vanished.

Adrian closed his eyes.

Tomás leaned forward.

“What operation?”

Celeste kept reading.

“Intercepting contact.”

Samuel watched her.

“Between whom?”

She traced one line with her finger.

“Nico and Sara.”

The room went still.

Marcel picked up his glass. It was empty. He set it down again.

Celeste read the dates. The first came only days after Sara's disappearance. The second three months later. Then another. And another.

Calls from pay phones, temporary numbers, intermediaries, and email accounts created to last only a few hours.

Attempts.

Not one.

Seventeen years of attempts.

Adrian stepped closer.

“She tried to contact him.”

Celeste did not need to answer.

Tomás looked toward the door Nico had left through.

“How many times?”

Celeste counted the lines on the first page, then the second.

“Twenty-three confirmed contacts.”

Samuel closed his eyes.

Adrian said, “And he never received any of them.”

“No.”

Celeste turned the page. There were short transcripts, voice messages converted to text.

**I need to know if you received what I sent.**

**I'm not safe, but I'm alive.**

**Don't trust anyone connected to the foundation.**

**Just say one word. I'll understand.**

Celeste stopped.

The final message had been sent six years earlier.

**I don't know if you're the one refusing to answer.**

The next line was marked in red.

**REPLY SENT FROM NICO SAINT'S ADDRESS**

Celeste felt her stomach tighten.

Adrian saw her expression.

“What is it?”

She did not answer.

Marcel watched her.

Tomás stood.

“Read it.”

Celeste kept her eyes on the page.

Samuel said her name.

She drew a breath and read.

“‘Don't contact me again.’”

Her voice was steady. Too steady.

“‘I made my choice. Make yours.’”

No one moved.

Nico's empty chair became the center of the room.

Adrian lowered his head. Samuel tightened his grip on Artur's box. Tomás looked at Marcel.

“It was sent in his name.”

Duarte answered, “Yes.”

“By Rui.”

“Yes.”

“With Marcel's authorization?”

Duarte looked at the King.

“Not for that specific message.”

Marcel opened his hands.

“Then it wasn't me.”

Celeste turned slowly.

“You authorized someone to control the contact between them.”

“I authorized surveillance.”

“Interception.”

“Protection.”

The word changed her face.

Celeste rose from the nameless chair.

“Don't use that word.”

Marcel stood too.

“She was a risk.”

“To whom?”

“To all of us.”

“To the Group.”

“Yes.”

“To you.”

“That too.”

Celeste held the page with both hands.

“And Nico?”

Marcel exhaled.

“He was unpredictable.”

“So you gave another man the power to decide what he was allowed to know.”

“We needed to contain the damage.”

Celeste laughed once, without humor.

“Of course.”

She sat again, not in the nameless chair but in her own place. The Queen lay beside her plate. The chair she had occupied remained empty.

Duarte watched the move.

“The function ended when you revoked the protection.”

Celeste did not respond.

Tomás moved closer to the table.

“Rui still works with Nico.”

“Yes,” Duarte said.

“All this time.”

“Yes.”

“Is he still being paid?”

“The regular payments ended three years ago.”

Marcel looked at him.

“Then we control nothing.”

Duarte held his gaze.

“I didn't say that.”

Samuel asked why the payments had stopped.

“Because Rui came to believe the relationship should continue in his own interest.”

Tomás breathed out.

“You no longer had to pay him.”

“Correct.”

Celeste read on. Contracts negotiated. Interviews refused. Projects redirected. People kept away from Nico.

Not only Sara.

Rui had built a border around him and presented it as a career.

“He shaped Nico's life.”

“In part,” Duarte replied.

Celeste raised her eyes.

“No. Don't use that now.”

Duarte fell silent.

“He opened doors that gave him control and closed the ones he couldn't monitor. He chose who came close, which contracts Nico accepted, which stories he told.”

Tomás looked at Marcel.

“Sounds familiar.”

Marcel said nothing.

Adrian picked up the photograph from the stage. Sara stood near the wings, almost outside the frame.

“Did she receive the reply?”

Duarte answered, “Yes.”

“So she believed Nico had rejected her.”

“For a time.”

“How long?”

“Years.”

Samuel bowed his head. Tomás ran a hand over his face.

“And then she found out?”

“She suspected.”

“When?”

“After the prosecutor identified transactions connected to Rui.”

Celeste looked up.

“The prosecutor knew.”

“Part of it.”

“And she couldn't reach Nico.”

“Not without exposing Sara.”

Celeste stared at the messages. For years, she had convinced herself silence could protect people.

Now she saw the result in numbered pages.

It was not absence.

It was architecture.

One person vanished from another's life, and in the space left behind someone built an explanation.

Duarte said, “There's more.”

Celeste turned the page.

There was a recent photograph of Rui Mendes leaving a restaurant, phone at his ear. On the back, a handwritten note:

**STILL HAS ACCESS TO NICO'S PHONE.**

Adrian moved beside her.

“That means—”

“He knows Nico is here,” Tomás said.

“Probably,” Duarte replied.

Samuel looked toward the door.

“And he knows Nico left the room.”

“Yes.”

Marcel leaned back.

“Then letting him leave was irresponsible.”

Celeste turned to him.

“Be quiet.”

She rarely raised her voice. She had not raised it now. Even so, the order crossed the table.

She turned to Samuel.

“Is the property gate still locked?”

“Yes.”

“He can't get out?”

“Not through the main gate.”

Tomás looked around.

“Is there another?”

Samuel hesitated.

Duarte noticed.

“Don't answer.”

Samuel faced him.

“You don't work for yourself.”

“Tonight, you did.”

“That is over.”

Samuel headed for the door.

“If you leave now,” Duarte said, “you won't return before the archive opens.”

Samuel stopped.

“Is that a rule?”

“It's a consequence.”

Nico was not there to laugh at the phrasing.

His absence made it worse.

Celeste stood.

“I'll go.”

Adrian caught her arm.

“We don't know where he is.”

“He's inside the house.”

“What if Rui has someone outside?”

“Then he shouldn't be alone.”

Marcel said, “You're not going.”

Celeste looked at her son's hand, not her husband. Adrian released her.

“I'll go with you.”

Duarte spoke.

“No one leaves.”

Tomás turned.

“You said we could leave the table.”

“You can.”

“Then?”

“The archive opens when the last person leaves their place.”

Samuel looked at him.

“And what happens?”

“You lose the initial access.”

Marcel straightened.

“Then we stay.”

Celeste watched how quickly he decided.

Samuel returned to the sideboard.

“How long?”

“Not long.”

“That's not an answer.”

Duarte looked at Artur's watch.

“Thirteen minutes.”

Adrian looked toward the door.

“And Nico?”

Duarte did not answer.

Celeste picked up Rui's photograph, then the messages. Her hand trembled. Barely.

Adrian saw it.

“Mother.”

She did not look up.

“I protected his dossier.”

“Yes.”

“I forced him to leave.”

“You didn't know.”

Celeste looked at her son.

“That sentence has done too much work in this family.”

Adrian fell silent.

“I saw a risk and decided for him. I wanted to protect Sara.”

“You did.”

“No. I wanted to protect the idea that I could still repair something.”

Samuel watched her.

“I didn't protect Nico. I used him to protect the version of myself I could still bear.”

Tomás lowered his eyes.

Duarte touched the deck.

“Recognition doesn't change the decision.”

“I know.”

“Then?”

Celeste removed Sara's photograph from the envelope.

“It changes the next one.”

She turned to Marcel.

“I want to know everything you authorized concerning Rui.”

Marcel laughed.

“You don't have the authority—”

She threw the sheet in front of him.

“Everything.”

The paper slid across the table.

Marcel looked at the messages.

“I don't remember.”

“That's a lie,” Celeste said.

Duarte did not correct her.

Marcel raised his eyes.

Celeste understood.

“You remember.”

“I remember a containment operation.”

“Name.”

“I don't know.”

“Who proposed it?”

Marcel looked at Duarte.

Celeste followed his gaze.

“Was it him?”

“No,” Duarte said.

“Then who?”

Marcel stayed silent.

Samuel moved closer.

“The name.”

Marcel looked at the knife in the center of the table, then at Samuel.

“Rui.”

Tomás frowned.

“He proposed his own hiring?”

“He contacted us.”

Celeste went still.

“When?”

“After the publication.”

“He said he could control Nico?”

Marcel clenched his jaw.

“He said he could keep him functional.”

Adrian closed his eyes.

Tomás gave a bitter laugh.

“Functional.”

Marcel continued.

“He was unstable. Drinking. Missing meetings. Refusing contracts.”

“Because Sara had disappeared.”

“Yes.”

“And Rui offered to use that.”

“He offered to manage the situation.”

Celeste shook her head.

“He offered to turn Nico's grief into a job.”

Marcel did not answer.

“Correct,” Duarte said.

Celeste turned to him.

“And you knew?”

“Later.”

“Did you do anything?”

“I kept evidence.”

“Naturally.”

“Yes.”

“To use.”

“Yes.”

Tomás looked at Duarte.

“Has anyone in this room ever found a truth and not thought first about its value?”

Samuel looked at his daughter's photograph. Adrian at his envelope. Celeste did not answer. Neither did Marcel.

Duarte did.

“No.”

The honesty did not help.

A door echoed on the floor above.

Everyone turned.

Not the front door.

An interior door.

Samuel went still.

“Someone's on the stairs.”

Marcel looked at Duarte.

“Security?”

“No.”

Footsteps began to descend. Slow. Unhidden.

Celeste held Sara's photograph. The scent of orange blossom seemed to return to the room before the person did.

Samuel watched the hallway. Adrian moved closer to his mother. Tomás stood. Marcel straightened his jacket. Duarte did not move.

A shadow appeared on the foyer wall.

Then a hand on the banister.

Nico appeared first.

He stopped halfway down the stairs, wearing his coat, the Joker between his fingers.

Celeste stood.

“Nico.”

He looked at the open envelope, then at the documents spread across the table.

“It took you less time than I expected.”

Celeste held the false message.

“There's more.”

“I know.”

She went still.

“You knew?”

“I found him.”

“Who?”

Nico descended another step.

“Rui.”

Marcel glanced toward the door.

“He was on my phone,” Nico continued.

Tomás frowned.

“How?”

“A missed call. Then another. Then a message asking if I was all right.”

Celeste looked at the recent photograph of Rui.

“He knows where you are.”

“Yes.”

“Did you speak to him?”

“No. I blocked the number.”

Duarte said, “That won't stop him from accessing the device.”

Nico looked at him.

“I don't have the device anymore.”

He removed the phone from his pocket. Its screen was cracked. He placed it on the step and crushed it beneath his shoe.

The glass snapped.

Marcel breathed out.

“Childish.”

“Possibly.” Nico looked at him. “But it was the first thing I did today without asking Rui.”

Samuel watched him.

“You shouldn't have come back.”

“I know.”

Duarte asked, “Then why?”

Nico looked at Celeste.

“Because someone opened the envelope.”

She tightened her grip on the documents.

“Nico—”

“No. Don't explain yet.”

Celeste closed her mouth.

Nico looked at Sara's photograph in her hands. His expression shifted. Barely.

“Is it all there?”

“I don't know.”

“Of course.”

He entered the dining room.

Duarte rose for the first time that night.

Nico stopped.

“That looks important.”

Duarte looked toward the hall.

Samuel did too.

Footsteps resumed upstairs.

Nico turned toward the staircase.

A woman appeared at the top.

Her hair was simply tied back, her coat dark, a small scar near her jaw.

Samuel lowered his eyes.

Celeste recognized her without ever having seen her.

Nico did not move.

Sara Almeida placed one hand on the banister.

She looked first at Duarte, then at Marcel, and finally at Nico.

Neither of them smiled.

She descended one step.

“It's time to finish dinner.”
