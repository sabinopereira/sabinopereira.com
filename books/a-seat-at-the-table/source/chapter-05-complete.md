# CHAPTER 5

Marcel kept his hand beside the King's envelope.

He did not touch it.

The aroma of roasted meat rose from the plate, mingling with reduced wine and burnt rosemary. Fat gleamed in the candlelight. A thin line of sauce traveled toward the edge of the porcelain.

Marcel looked at Celeste.

“You already knew.”

She did not answer.

“You knew it would be me.”

“We all knew.”

“That isn't what I asked.”

Celeste aligned the transcript with the envelope.

“Then ask a better question.”

Tomás looked away.

Adrian remained seated, but he no longer seemed to occupy the same space as his father. He had moved back a few inches, enough to turn proximity into distance.

Nico watched Marcel.

The blue card remained face down before him.

Marcel turned to the Dealer.

“What is the point?”

“Of the envelope?”

“Of tonight.”

“We will get there.”

“I don't like delayed answers.”

“You do when you're the one delaying them.”

Marcel smiled without humor.

“You're very comfortable in a house that isn't yours.”

“Comfort is not ownership.”

“That isn't what I said.”

The Dealer set down the deck.

“No.”

Marcel looked around.

The walls.

The curtains.

The side doors.

Samuel standing motionless beside the sideboard.

“Whose house is this?”

“That is not yet relevant.”

“Everything is relevant when someone prevents me from leaving.”

“The dining room door is open.”

“And the gate?”

“It is not.”

“Then I'm locked in.”

“Yes.”

Marcel released a quiet breath.

“Finally.”

“Finally what?”

“A direct answer.”

“You asked a direct question.”

Nico picked up his glass.

“It's almost educational.”

Marcel looked at him.

“You seem far too pleased.”

“I'm not.”

“Then why are you smiling?”

Nico touched his own face as though checking.

“Manufacturing defect.”

Marcel returned to the envelope.

“If I don't open it?”

“The dossier is released.”

“To whom?”

“The authorities, the press, and the people directly affected.”

“And if I open it?”

“It remains in this house until the end of dinner.”

“That's all?”

“That's all.”

“No hidden consequences?”

“There will be other choices later.”

“Which ones?”

“We have not reached them.”

Marcel rested his forearms on the table.

“The problem with men like you is that you believe controlling information is the same as controlling people.”

The Dealer tilted his head slightly.

“The problem with men like you is that you have proven it too many times.”

Marcel broke the seal.

He removed two pages and a photograph.

He looked at the photograph first.

His face did not change.

He placed it facedown.

Read the first page.

Then the second.

He carefully folded both sheets in half and placed them over his plate. One corner touched the sauce.

Celeste noticed.

So did Nico.

Marcel picked up his knife.

He cut a piece of meat.

Adrian looked at him.

“Aren't you going to say anything?”

Marcel put the food in his mouth.

“There is nothing to say.”

The Dealer did not correct him.

Tomás leaned forward.

“Does that mean it's true?”

The Dealer replied, “It means only that Marcel did not call it false.”

Marcel set down the knife.

“You choose your words very carefully.”

“Yes.”

“It's a form of cowardice.”

“Sometimes.”

The admission made him look up.

Marcel picked up the photograph again.

He turned it over.

It showed a middle-aged man standing outside an office building. He held a dark leather briefcase in one hand and shielded his eyes from the sun with the other.

An ordinary photograph.

Celeste looked away.

Adrian saw.

“Who is he?”

Marcel put down the photograph.

“No one.”

Celeste closed her eyes.

The Dealer spoke.

“Artur Leal.”

Samuel did not move.

Only his right hand closed beside his body.

Nico noticed.

Adrian looked at his father.

“Who was he?”

“An employee.”

“Deputy chief financial officer,” the Dealer corrected.

Marcel leaned back.

“The distinction doesn't matter.”

“It mattered to him.”

“Why?”

“Because the position gave him access.”

Tomás stopped eating.

“To what?”

The Dealer looked at the pages stained with sauce.

“Internal transfers. Intermediary companies. Payments with no declared source.”

Marcel pressed his lips together.

“It was all investigated.”

“No.”

“It was.”

“It was closed.”

“That is how an investigation ends when it finds no evidence.”

Nico spoke.

“Or when the evidence finds an exit.”

Marcel looked at him.

“You have no idea what you're talking about.”

“No.”

Nico pointed to the envelope.

“But you do.”

Marcel turned to Adrian.

“You don't need to hear this.”

His son released a short breath.

“That decision is no longer yours.”

“I'm your father.”

“And that no longer ends every conversation.”

Marcel tightened his grip on the knife.

Celeste watched his hand.

The Dealer spoke.

“Artur prepared a report.”

“A draft.”

“A report.”

“With errors.”

“With evidence.”

Marcel leaned closer.

“You don't know the context.”

“I know the numbers.”

“Numbers are not context.”

“No.”

The Dealer rested two fingers on the deck.

“But a transfer does not cease to exist because someone calls it strategy.”

Marcel gave a quiet laugh.

“It's easy to talk when you've never been responsible for thousands of jobs.”

Tomás replied, “Artur was responsible for his too.”

Marcel turned.

“Stay out of this.”

Tomás looked at his own envelope.

“I've spent the evening hearing what I did to earn a place.”

He pointed at the photograph.

“He lost his for doing the opposite.”

Marcel's face hardened.

“Artur did not lose his job for reporting anything.”

The Dealer replied, “True.”

Adrian frowned.

“Then what happened?”

The Dealer kept his eyes on Marcel.

“Artur died.”

The silence arrived before the reaction.

Celeste placed her fork on the plate.

Tomás lowered his eyes.

Nico stopped touching his glass.

Adrian looked at his father.

Marcel held his posture.

“It was an accident.”

The Dealer replied, “Yes.”

The confirmation seemed to return Marcel to his chair.

He opened one hand.

“Then we're finished.”

“No.”

“You just confirmed it was an accident.”

“It was.”

“Therefore, I was not responsible.”

The Dealer did not answer.

Marcel exhaled.

“Is that what this is? Taking a tragedy and turning it into an accusation?”

“No.”

“Then explain.”

The Dealer looked at Samuel.

Samuel left the wall.

He walked to the sideboard and removed a small box of dark wood.

He placed it in the center of the table.

Marcel looked at it.

Celeste went still.

“I know that box,” she said.

Adrian turned.

“From where?”

“The office.”

Marcel did not look at his wife.

“You don't know that.”

“There were two like it.”

The Dealer replied, “Three.”

Celeste frowned.

“Three?”

Marcel kept his gaze on the box.

The Dealer indicated it.

“Open it.”

“No.”

“You may refuse.”

Marcel looked at him.

“And will it be released?”

“No.”

“Then I won't open it.”

The Dealer turned to Adrian.

“You may.”

Marcel answered at once.

“No.”

Adrian studied the box.

“Why?”

“Because it does not concern you.”

“A man died.”

“It does not concern you.”

Adrian stood.

Marcel stood too.

“Don't touch it.”

The Dealer remained seated.

Adrian looked at his father.

“You're afraid of a box.”

“I'm telling you to sit down.”

“No.”

Samuel stepped closer.

Marcel saw him.

Adrian reached for the lid.

Marcel seized his wrist.

Celeste stood.

“Marcel.”

He did not hear her.

“Let go of me,” Adrian said.

“Don't open it.”

“Why?”

“Because I said so.”

Adrian tried to free himself.

“That isn't enough anymore.”

Marcel tightened his grip.

Nico stood.

“Let him go.”

Marcel looked at him.

“Stay out of this.”

“You've said that too many times.”

Samuel took another step.

Marcel released his son.

Adrian opened the box.

Inside was a broken watch.

The glass was cracked. The hands had stopped at ten twenty-three.

Celeste raised a hand to her mouth.

Marcel sat again.

Adrian picked up the watch.

“Was it his?”

“Yes,” the Dealer replied.

“Artur's?”

“Yes.”

“Why is it broken?”

No one answered immediately.

Celeste spoke.

“He fell on the service stairs.”

Marcel looked at her.

“No.”

“That is what they said.”

“That is what happened.”

The Dealer replied, “He fell.”

Adrian placed the watch inside the box.

“At what time?”

“Ten twenty-three.”

Adrian looked at the hands.

Tomás asked, “Did he die immediately?”

The room changed.

Marcel picked up his glass.

His hand remained steady.

The Dealer answered.

“No.”

Celeste closed her eyes.

Adrian looked at his father.

“How long was he alive?”

Marcel drank.

“We don't know precisely,” the Dealer said. “Between seven and eleven minutes.”

Adrian stepped away from the table.

“The report said he died in the fall.”

Marcel put down the glass.

“That is what I was told.”

The Dealer looked at him.

“No.”

Marcel tightened his fingers.

“I wasn't there when they found him.”

“You were there when he fell.”

Celeste opened her eyes.

Tomás went still.

Nico watched Marcel.

Adrian took a breath.

“You were there?”

Marcel did not answer.

The Dealer spoke.

“Yes.”

Marcel stood.

“Enough.”

“Artur requested a meeting.”

“Enough.”

“He brought the report.”

“I said enough.”

“You argued.”

Marcel struck the table with his hand.

The glasses shook.

“Enough.”

Samuel moved.

Only one step.

Marcel saw.

The Dealer continued.

“Artur left the office.”

“Yes.”

“You followed him.”

“No.”

“You did.”

Marcel pointed at him.

“You don't know.”

“There is a recording.”

The color left Marcel's face.

Celeste watched him.

Adrian understood.

“A recording of what?”

Marcel looked at the Dealer.

“You said you collected facts.”

“Yes.”

“You have no authorization to…”

“You did not ask whether I had authorization.”

Nico spoke.

“It seems late for bureaucracy.”

Marcel ignored him.

The Dealer made a gesture.

Samuel switched on a small device.

The screen beside the curtains lit up.

The image showed a service corridor.

No sound.

Artur appeared first.

He walked quickly, holding the briefcase close to his body.

Marcel appeared several seconds later.

He caught up with him beside the stairwell door.

They argued.

Artur tried to move away.

Marcel put a hand on his shoulder.

He did not push him.

Artur wrenched himself free.

Stepped back.

His foot missed the first stair.

He disappeared from the frame.

The recording continued.

Marcel stood motionless beside the door.

He looked down.

He did not descend.

He waited.

Then he closed the door.

The image ended.

No one spoke.

Marcel stared at the dark screen.

Adrian had gone pale.

“He was alive.”

Marcel did not answer.

“When you closed the door.”

“I didn't know.”

The Dealer replied, “You did.”

Marcel turned.

“No.”

“Artur called your name.”

“I didn't hear him.”

“He called twice.”

“I didn't hear him.”

“The original recording has sound.”

Samuel placed another device on the table.

Marcel stepped forward.

“No.”

The Dealer looked at him.

Marcel stopped.

“It isn't necessary.”

Adrian replied, “It is.”

“No.”

“I want to hear it.”

Marcel turned to his son.

“You don't know what you're asking.”

“I want to hear it.”

Celeste closed her eyes.

The Dealer made a gesture.

Samuel pressed the button.

First, the argument.

Artur accused Marcel of moving funds and using companies to buy decisions.

Marcel told him he did not understand the impact a report would have.

Then came the sound of the fall.

A heavy blow.

Silence.

Artur's breathing.

His voice came weakly.

“Marcel.”

The recording continued.

“Marcel, help me.”

A noise.

The door closing.

End.

Adrian stepped away from the table.

Tomás lowered his head.

Celeste's eyes were wet.

Nico did not look at Marcel.

Samuel switched off the device.

Marcel remained standing.

“I didn't push him.”

No one answered.

“I didn't push him.”

Adrian looked at him.

“But you left him.”

“I didn't know if…”

“He asked for help.”

“I was in shock.”

The Dealer replied, “True.”

Marcel turned.

The confirmation gave him air.

“You heard him.”

The Dealer continued.

“You were in shock for several seconds.”

Marcel went still.

“Then you thought about the report.”

“No.”

“You thought about the company.”

“No.”

“You thought about what would happen if someone found Artur alive and heard him speak.”

“No.”

The Dealer held his gaze.

“You returned to the office.”

Marcel clenched his fists.

“I called Celeste.”

Everyone looked at her.

Celeste did not move.

Adrian spoke.

“That was when you canceled the call.”

She closed her eyes.

“Yes.”

Marcel pointed at his wife.

“It was her.”

Celeste opened her eyes.

“No.”

“You canceled it.”

“After you told me he was dead.”

Marcel fell silent.

“You said there was nothing left to be done.”

“I believed…”

“No.”

Celeste shook her head.

“You said what you needed me to believe.”

Marcel looked around.

“It was an accident.”

Nico replied, “The fall was.”

Marcel turned.

Nico continued.

“The rest was a choice.”

“You don't know what you would have done.”

“No.”

Nico looked at the watch.

“That's the difference between us right now.”

Marcel stepped closer.

“What is?”

“I'm still afraid to find out.”

Marcel laughed.

His voice came out hoarse.

“You think you're better.”

“No.”

“Then tell me what I am.”

Nico fell silent.

Marcel moved closer.

“Call me a murderer.”

“No.”

“Because you don't have the courage?”

“Because the word helps you.”

Marcel frowned.

Nico pointed at the screen.

“If I call you a murderer, you can argue the definition.”

Marcel went still.

“What you did does not need a name.”

Nico looked toward the dining room door.

“You found an injured man. You heard him ask for help. You closed the door because his truth was more dangerous to you than his death.”

Marcel did not answer.

“That's enough.”

The silence grew heavy.

The Dealer asked, “Do you want me to stop?”

Marcel looked at him.

“You've already shown everything.”

“No.”

Celeste raised her eyes.

Adrian went still.

Tomás watched the envelope.

Nico looked at Samuel.

His hand was closed again.

Marcel saw it.

“Who are you?”

Samuel did not answer.

“I asked who you are.”

The Dealer spoke.

“We have not reached coffee yet.”

Marcel gave an empty laugh.

“Everything has a course.”

“Yes.”

“Even the dead.”

“Especially the dead.”

Marcel looked at the broken watch.

“You want me to admit responsibility?”

“No.”

“Then what?”

“I want you to choose.”

The nameless chair seemed to have moved closer.

Marcel looked at it.

“Choose what?”

“The fate of your dossier.”

“I thought it stayed private if I continued.”

“Until the end of dinner.”

“And then?”

The Dealer looked toward the empty chair.

“One person will have limited authority over the archive.”

“Whoever sits there.”

“Yes.”

“Can they destroy the dossiers?”

“They may protect one.”

Marcel frowned.

“One?”

“Yes.”

“And the rest?”

“They will be decided later.”

“By whom?”

The Dealer did not answer.

Marcel looked at the others.

Celeste.

Adrian.

Tomás.

Nico.

Then back at the chair.

Fear slowly disappeared from his face.

Calculation took its place.

Nico saw.

“You're already thinking about sitting there.”

Marcel looked at him.

“I'm thinking about the consequences.”

“That's what you call desire when there are witnesses.”

Marcel did not answer.

The Dealer rested a hand on the deck.

“Do you want the dossier to remain on the table?”

Marcel looked at the pages stained with sauce.

The photograph.

The watch.

The envelope.

“Yes.”

Adrian raised his eyes.

Celeste remained still.

The Dealer asked, “Even knowing someone else may release it?”

Marcel looked at the chair.

“Yes.”

“Why?”

Marcel sat again.

He picked up the knife.

Cut a piece of meat.

“Because I intend to influence the decision.”

Nico released a quiet breath.

The Dealer inclined his head.

“Continue.”

Marcel put the meat in his mouth.

Chewed.

Swallowed.

Samuel approached to clear the plates.

When he reached Marcel, he observed the nearly untouched food.

“Are you finished, sir?”

Marcel continued looking at the chair.

“For now.”

Samuel removed the plate.

Then he picked up the box containing the watch.

Marcel placed a hand on the lid.

“It stays.”

Samuel stopped.

Marcel looked at the broken watch.

“It's mine.”

“No.”

It was his first direct refusal.

Marcel raised his eyes.

“It's in my dossier.”

“It does not belong to you.”

“Then to whom?”

Samuel placed his hand on the box.

“The family.”

The two men looked at one another.

Marcel took several seconds to remove his hand.

Samuel carried the box away.

The side doors opened.

The aroma of dessert entered the room.

Dark chocolate.

Orange.

Burnt sugar.

Nico looked at the blue card before his plate.

Samuel placed the dessert in front of him.

Then he laid a blue envelope over the card.

The Dealer stopped shuffling.

“Now,” he said, “we can talk about you.”
