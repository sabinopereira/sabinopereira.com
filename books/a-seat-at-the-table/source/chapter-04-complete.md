# CHAPTER 4

Celeste kept one finger on the Queen's seal.

She did not open it.

The fish before her released a faint scent of brown butter and crushed herbs. Its golden skin gleamed beneath the candles. The plate looked prepared for a photograph, not to be eaten.

The Dealer waited.

Marcel picked up his glass.

Adrian kept the Ace's envelope beside his plate, perfectly aligned with the silverware. Tomás had left his own slightly to one side, as though moving it a few inches might make it less true.

Nico watched Celeste.

Not the envelope.

She noticed.

“Is there a problem?”

“No.”

“Then stop looking.”

“I could be looking at the fish.”

“You aren't.”

Nico picked up the bread.

“I am now.”

Celeste turned to the Dealer.

“My son has just discovered that part of his life was arranged by other people.”

“Yes.”

“And he decided not to release the dossier in order to protect the people who were harmed.”

“Yes.”

“What do you expect me to do now?”

“Open it.”

“That wasn't what I asked.”

The Dealer placed his hands on the table.

“I expect you to choose knowing that your choice will also affect people who are absent.”

Celeste looked at the envelope.

“That isn't an answer.”

“It is the answer available.”

Marcel released a quiet breath.

“You're beginning to repeat yourself.”

The Dealer looked at him.

“Some people make it necessary.”

Celeste broke the seal.

The black wax split along a clean line.

She removed two pages.

The first contained a list.

The second, a transcript.

She read the list to the end.

Her expression did not change.

She folded the second page without opening it and set both beside her plate.

Marcel watched her.

“What is it?”

“A list.”

Tomás looked at his own envelope.

“Lists aren't having a very good evening.”

Celeste picked up her glass.

“That depends on who is reading them.”

The Dealer replied, “You wrote it.”

She put down the glass.

“I didn't ask you to answer.”

“I did not answer for you.”

“You just did.”

“I corrected the document's origin.”

Celeste looked at Marcel.

“It contains nothing you don't already know.”

He remained still.

Adrian looked from one to the other.

“You know what's on there?”

Marcel leaned back.

“I don't know what she's referring to.”

The Dealer spoke.

“That is not true.”

Celeste let out a small breath.

Almost a laugh.

“It's curious to watch someone else correct you.”

Adrian leaned toward the table.

“What kind of list is it?”

Celeste placed her hand over the first page.

“Private information.”

Tomás glanced at the Dealer.

“That phrase should pay rent.”

Celeste looked at him.

“You sold names for a promise. You may not be the right person to discuss privacy.”

Tomás absorbed the sentence without responding.

Adrian persisted.

“Information about whom?”

“People.”

“How many?”

Celeste did not answer.

The Dealer said, “One hundred twenty-three.”

The number seemed larger because he spoke it without emphasis.

Adrian went still.

Tomás looked at Marcel.

Nico continued watching Celeste.

She looked at the Dealer.

“They weren't one hundred twenty-three people.”

“They were one hundred twenty-three records.”

“That isn't the same.”

Nico spoke.

“I imagine it helped to think of them that way.”

Celeste turned.

“To think what?”

“That they were records.”

“They were.”

“They were also people.”

She held his gaze.

“You know nothing about my work.”

“I didn't know this was work.”

Marcel interrupted.

“Enough.”

Celeste looked at him.

“No.”

The word came quietly.

Marcel fell silent.

Adrian pointed to the page.

“What kind of information?”

Celeste opened it again.

She read without looking at anyone.

“Conversations. Debts. Affairs. Addictions. Diagnoses. Disciplinary proceedings. Payments. Undisclosed meetings.”

Tomás gave a short whistle.

Celeste continued.

“Promises made in private. Agreements that officially did not exist. Opinions that could not be repeated in public.”

Adrian frowned.

“You kept everything?”

“I kept what was necessary.”

“Necessary for what?”

She did not answer immediately.

Marcel did it for her.

“Protection.”

Celeste turned to him.

“Don't speak for me.”

“That was what it was for.”

“For you.”

“For the family.”

She tilted her head slightly.

“Family remains a very useful word.”

Adrian looked at his parents.

“Did you use the information?”

No one answered.

The Dealer spoke.

“Eighteen times.”

Celeste closed her eyes for a moment.

Tomás stopped touching his glass.

Adrian asked, “Eighteen people?”

Celeste opened her eyes.

“Yes.”

“You blackmailed eighteen people?”

“No.”

The Dealer answered.

“True.”

Adrian looked confused.

“Then what?”

Celeste folded her hands.

“I didn't ask for money.”

Tomás gave a quiet laugh.

“That changes everything.”

She ignored him.

“I did not demand personal favors.”

Nico tilted his head.

“Only professional ones.”

Celeste looked at him.

“Some.”

“How many?”

“I don't know.”

The Dealer spoke.

“You do.”

Celeste pressed her thumb against her ring.

A minimal gesture.

The only part of her that moved.

“Twelve.”

Adrian drew slightly away.

“What did you force those people to do?”

Celeste looked at the list.

“Withdraw proposals. Change votes. Delay investigations. Hire someone. Fire someone.”

Tomás glanced at Marcel.

“Who?”

“It doesn't concern you.”

“Maybe I was one of them.”

Celeste looked at him.

“You weren't important enough.”

Tomás went still.

The answer had seemed automatic.

Celeste understood too late.

So did Nico.

Adrian continued.

“Why?”

She released her ring.

“Because the decisions were already being manipulated.”

“That doesn't answer the question.”

“It does.”

“No.”

Adrian leaned forward.

“Why did you think you had the right?”

Celeste looked at her son.

“Because I was in rooms where powerful men made decisions that destroyed thousands of lives and called it strategy.”

Marcel did not react.

“Because I heard what they said when they believed no woman was truly listening.”

She looked at the men around the table.

“I learned early that truth without power was only a sad story.”

Nico spoke.

“So you stored power.”

“Yes.”

“And called it protection.”

“Sometimes it was.”

“For whom?”

Celeste fell silent.

Nico continued.

“For the people affected by those decisions?”

“Some of them.”

“For you?”

“That too.”

“For Marcel?”

She looked away.

She did not need to answer.

Adrian understood.

“You used information to protect Dad.”

Celeste turned toward him.

“I protected more than your father.”

“Who?”

“The company.”

Marcel straightened.

“We don't need to discuss…”

Celeste cut him off.

“Eighteen times.”

Marcel fell silent.

She touched the page.

“Twelve of those decisions were made to protect the Voss Group.”

Adrian looked at his father.

“Did you know?”

Marcel kept his face still.

Celeste answered.

“He knew enough.”

“That isn't an answer.”

“It's the only one you'll get from him.”

Marcel picked up the wine.

Adrian looked at his mother.

“And the other six?”

Celeste folded one corner of the page.

She immediately flattened it.

“They weren't for Marcel.”

“Then for whom?”

She looked at the names.

“People who couldn't protect themselves.”

Tomás exhaled.

“Convenient.”

Celeste turned toward him.

“A woman was going to lose custody of her daughter because her husband had friends at the courthouse.”

Tomás went quiet.

“A journalist received photographs of his son leaving school.”

Nico stopped moving the bread.

“A doctor was forced to falsify a report.”

Samuel remained by the wall.

His right hand slowly closed.

Celeste continued.

“An employee discovered internal transfers and tried to report them.”

Marcel went still.

Artur's name was not spoken.

Even so, it occupied the room.

Adrian spoke.

“Then you did good things.”

Celeste looked at him.

“No.”

“You just said…”

“I did useful things.”

Her voice dropped.

“That isn't the same.”

The Dealer watched her.

Celeste continued.

“Some people stayed alive. Some kept their children. Others kept their jobs.”

She ran her fingers over the list.

“And others were simply moved.”

Adrian frowned.

“Moved?”

“Out of an investigation. Into a position. Away from a witness.”

Tomás rested his arms on the table.

“People as pieces.”

Celeste looked at him.

“Everyone in this room has treated someone like a piece.”

Nico replied, “That doesn't make the board innocent.”

She held his gaze.

Then she looked at the second page, still folded.

The Dealer spoke.

“The list is not the important part.”

Celeste did not move.

Marcel looked at her.

“What's on there?”

“Nothing.”

The Dealer answered.

“A lie.”

The word crossed the room without volume.

Celeste held the folded page.

Adrian saw the fear before she could hide it.

“Mom?”

She opened the transcript.

Read the first line.

Closed it again.

“This should not exist.”

The Dealer replied, “It does.”

“How did you get it?”

He did not answer.

Celeste looked at Samuel.

“Was it you?”

“No, ma'am.”

She believed him.

The fear did not diminish.

Marcel leaned forward.

“Show me.”

Celeste pressed the page against her body.

“No.”

“Celeste.”

“No.”

He stood.

Nico spoke.

“I'd stay seated.”

Marcel turned.

“Stay out of this.”

“She moved back when you came closer.”

Marcel looked at his wife.

Celeste realized Nico was right.

Marcel slowly returned to his chair.

“Read it.”

She took a deep breath.

“No.”

The Dealer asked, “Do you want me to stop?”

Celeste looked at him.

“If I say yes, the dossier is released.”

“Yes.”

“If I continue, it remains in this house until the end.”

“Yes.”

She gave a small laugh.

“You call that a choice?”

“Yes.”

“Of course.”

She opened the transcript again.

“Recorded conversation. March twenty-eighth. Twelve forty-six a.m.”

She stopped.

Nico watched her.

Adrian pressed his hands against the table.

Celeste continued.

“Male voice: ‘The investigation cannot reach the commission.’”

Marcel went still.

“Female voice: ‘It won't.’”

Adrian looked at his mother.

“Male voice: ‘How can you be so sure?’”

Celeste tightened her grip on the page.

“Female voice: ‘Because the commission chairman has a daughter.’”

Tomás lowered his eyes.

“Male voice: ‘I don't want the details.’”

Celeste's voice weakened.

“Female voice: ‘That's why you have me.’”

Marcel looked away.

Adrian lost all color.

Celeste continued.

“Male voice: ‘Handle it.’”

She stopped.

The Dealer said, “Continue.”

Celeste looked at him.

Anger helped her recover her voice.

“Female voice: ‘And what do I do with the woman?’”

Marcel closed his eyes.

“Male voice: ‘The same thing you did with the others.’”

Celeste lowered the page.

No one spoke.

Adrian looked at his father.

“That was you.”

Marcel replied, “You can't prove it.”

The Dealer spoke.

“It is his voice.”

Marcel clenched his jaw.

Adrian turned to his mother.

“Who was the woman?”

Celeste did not answer.

“Who?”

She closed her eyes.

“A prosecutor.”

Tomás stopped breathing.

“What did you do to her?”

Celeste opened her eyes.

“I discovered that her husband was hiding money.”

“And?”

“I showed her part of it.”

“To make her drop the investigation?”

“Yes.”

“Did she?”

Celeste looked at Marcel.

“No.”

The answer surprised the table.

Adrian leaned closer.

“Then what happened?”

“She was removed.”

“How?”

“An anonymous complaint.”

“Against her husband?”

“No.”

“Against her?”

“Yes.”

“Accusing her of what?”

Celeste held the transcript with both hands.

“Misconduct.”

Tomás exhaled.

“Was it true?”

“Part of it.”

Nico spoke.

“Another portion.”

Celeste looked at him.

“Yes.”

“And the rest?”

“It was constructed.”

Adrian pushed back his chair.

“By you?”

Celeste fell silent.

The Dealer answered.

“Yes.”

Adrian stood.

“You destroyed a woman's career to protect the company.”

Celeste stood as well.

“It wasn't that simple.”

“It was exactly that simple.”

“No.”

“She was investigating crimes.”

“She was getting close to people who would have destroyed her anyway.”

“So you did it first.”

Celeste went still.

Adrian continued.

“Did you call it protection?”

“No.”

“Necessity?”

She did not answer.

“Strategy?”

Celeste looked at the page.

“Cowardice.”

The word emerged without defense.

Marcel looked at her.

Perhaps he expected an explanation that would protect him too.

None came.

Celeste sat again.

Her posture remained straight. Her shoulders aligned. Only her breathing revealed the effort.

Adrian remained standing.

“What happened to the prosecutor?”

Celeste looked at the Dealer.

He answered.

“She disappeared from public life. Changed her name. Left the country.”

Nico raised his eyes.

“Is she alive?”

“Yes.”

Celeste closed her eyes.

Adrian watched her.

“Did you know?”

“No.”

“So tonight is the first time you've found out.”

“Yes.”

Adrian slowly returned to his seat.

Celeste laid the transcript on the table.

Tomás was looking at her.

“Was she one of the six?”

“Yes.”

“One of the people you said you protected.”

Celeste ran a finger along the edge of the page.

“At the time, I told myself she was.”

Nico tilted his head.

“Maybe she was.”

Celeste looked at him.

“No.”

“She's alive.”

“She lost her career. Her name. Her country.”

“Sometimes survival means losing almost everything.”

Celeste held his gaze.

“That does not absolve me.”

“No.”

“Then why say it?”

“Because turning everything into one thing is also a form of control.”

She fell silent.

Nico continued.

“You may have saved her from some people and destroyed her for other reasons.”

Celeste looked at the transcript.

“Yes.”

“Both can be true.”

“Yes.”

Marcel gave a quiet laugh.

“How touching.”

Celeste turned.

“No.”

He went still.

“You will not do that.”

“Do what?”

“Turn my admission into a weakness you can use later.”

Marcel leaned back.

“I don't need to.”

Celeste looked at him.

“You've needed to your entire life.”

Adrian raised his eyes.

Celeste continued.

“You used me because I could enter rooms you could not enter without looking like a threat.”

Marcel pressed his lips together.

“I used your intelligence.”

“No.”

She shook her head.

“You used my ability to do harm without leaving marks.”

Silence returned.

The Dealer asked, “Do you want me to stop?”

Celeste looked at the envelope.

Then at Adrian.

Tomás.

Nico.

Samuel by the wall.

Finally, Marcel.

“No.”

“Continue?”

“Yes.”

“Why?”

Celeste picked up her fork.

She cut a small piece of the fish, now lukewarm.

“I spent years deciding which truths other people could endure.”

She put the fish in her mouth.

Chewed.

Swallowed.

“It's time I stopped deciding.”

Samuel approached to clear the plates.

When he reached her, he looked at the nearly untouched fish.

“Are you finished, ma'am?”

Celeste set down the fork.

“No.”

Samuel waited.

She cut another piece.

She ate without looking away from Marcel.

He did not touch his own plate.

Neither did Adrian.

Celeste ate slowly.

Not from hunger.

From refusal.

Only when she set down the fork a second time did Samuel remove the plate.

The envelope remained beside her.

Nico looked at the transcript.

“Is the prosecutor's name on there?”

Celeste read the page again.

“No.”

“Why?”

The Dealer answered.

“Because it will matter later.”

Nico looked at him.

“For what?”

“Dessert.”

Celeste raised her eyes.

The Dealer did not explain.

The side doors opened.

The next course arrived in the service elevator.

Roasted meat.

Reduced wine.

Burnt rosemary.
