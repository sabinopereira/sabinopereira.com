Author's Note

Bad Girl Gospel: The Story of Naomi began as a question: What if heartless was not who she was, but the name the world gave to a woman who refused to stay hungry in order to be called good? Naomi is a fictional character, but her contradictions are human. She wants security without becoming a machine. She wants power without becoming cruel. She wants love without becoming dependent. She wants God without pretending she does not care about money, control, or the fear of losing everything. This book is not about excusing every wound. It is about telling the truth about what wounds can become.

Some women are called soft when they are starving. Some are called selfish when they finally eat. Some are called heartless when they stop offering their bodies, time, ambition, and future as proof of goodness. Naomi stands in that accusation. A baddie is what they see. A bad girl is what they judge. A free woman is what she is trying to become.
