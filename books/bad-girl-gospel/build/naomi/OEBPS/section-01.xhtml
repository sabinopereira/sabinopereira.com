Fiction Note

This is a fictional literary work. Naomi is not written as a perfect victim or a complete villain. She is a character built from contradiction, hunger, shame, ambition, fear, control, tenderness, and the difficult work of becoming honest. This book does not celebrate cruelty. It asks what happens when survival begins to look like greed, and when a woman who once had nothing decides she will never be powerless again. Some wounds make people softer. Some wounds make people strategic. Some make them hard to love before they learn how to feel safe. Naomi lives in that tension.

This is not a story about money alone. It is a story about what money comes to mean when you have known instability, humiliation, dependence, and the quiet violence of never having enough. It is also a story about the names women are given when they stop confusing sacrifice with holiness.
