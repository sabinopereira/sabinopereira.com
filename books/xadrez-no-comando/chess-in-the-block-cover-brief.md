# Chess in the Block - Cover Brief

## Visual Direction

Use the attached "Quiet Power" style as inspiration, but create a distinct cover
for "Chess in the Block".

The mood should be serious, cinematic, urban, and reflective.

## Core Look

- Dark black and charcoal background
- Large distressed gold title typography
- Chess pieces in the foreground, preferably a king centered
- Blurred city skyline or housing blocks in the background
- Wet pavement or reflective chessboard surface
- Subtle scratches, dust, grain, and worn texture
- Minimal color palette: black, charcoal, grey, muted gold
- Strong contrast, dramatic lighting

## Suggested Front Cover Text

CHESS  
IN THE  
BLOCK

Sabino Pereira

Optional subtitle:

Survival. Silence. Strategy.

Alternative subtitle:

Learning how to move without losing yourself.

## Composition Notes

The title should dominate the upper half of the cover. The chess piece should
sit in the lower center, large enough to feel symbolic but not so large that it
competes with the title.

The background should suggest the block without becoming too literal or busy.
Use blurred buildings, street lights, or public housing silhouettes rather than
clear documentary detail.

The cover should feel like literary reflection, not a chess manual.

## Avoid

- Bright colors
- Cartoon chess pieces
- Clean corporate design
- Overly literal street imagery
- Weapons, police tape, or crime-focused imagery
- Making it look like a chess strategy book
