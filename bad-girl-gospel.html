A woman behind the rumor.

Selah is desired, judged, guarded, spiritual, complicated, and still trying to understand where the woman ends and the character begins.

A baddie is what they see. A bad girl is what they judge. A free woman is what she becomes.

They called Selah a baddie when they wanted her body. They called her a bad girl when they could no longer control her heart. But nobody asked what it cost her to become untouchable.

The Image Beauty becomes power, prison, armor, and the first story people think they understand.
The Prayer Faith appears without performance, in bathrooms, after midnight, with guilt still on the tongue.
The Exit Freedom arrives when survival stops needing to look glamorous and starts telling the truth.

What kind of book is this?

Bad Girl Gospel: The Story of Selah is poetic literary fiction, spiritual noir, and confessional fiction for readers drawn to intimate character studies about women, desire, faith, guilt, power, reputation, and the cost of becoming free.

It is not a conventional romance or an erotica title. It is a dark, reflective story about the woman behind the rumor, the performance of being untouchable, and the moment survival has to become truth.

Opening Manifesto

My name is Selah.

I am not your lesson, your warning, or your rehabilitation project.

You called me a baddie when you wanted my body. You called me a bad girl when you could no longer control my heart.

You never asked what it cost me to become this untouchable.

I am not cold. I am just done bleeding in public.

Yes, I like money. Money means keys, exits, silence, distance.

Yes, I like attention. I grew up invisible.

Yes, I like power. I remember too well what it felt like to beg.

Don’t worship me. Understand me, or move.

I still talk to God. In bathrooms. In Ubers. With glitter on my skin and guilt on my tongue.

My faith was never a performance for you.

A baddie is what you see. A bad girl is what you judge. A free woman is what I am becoming.

My name is Selah. And this is my gospel.
