The premise

What does it mean to choose someone again?

Every ten years, couples must review their relationship. The system does not ask whether love once existed. It asks what they still want to be to one another now.

Tomás and Leonor have built a home, raised a son, and learned how to keep a life running. What they have not learned is whether remaining together is still an act of love—or merely the path of least resistance.

Their Identity Review forces them to confront desire, sacrifice, temptation, money, parenthood, and the uncomfortable possibility that two people can love each other while no longer choosing the same life.
